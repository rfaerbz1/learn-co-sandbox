Chien-Shiung Wu

Chien-Shiung Wu was a Chineese-American physicist who made significant contributions to the field of nuclear physics.


Birth

Chien-Shiung Wu was born in Liuhe, China on May 31, 1912.

Death

Chien-Shiung Wu died February 16, 1997 at the age of 84 in New York City, New York.

Scientific Contributions

In 1944 Chien-Shiung Wu joined the Manhattan Project at the lab at Columbia University, she focused on radiation detectors.

After the war, Chien-Shiung Wu investigated beta decay, which is when the nucleus of one element changes into another element. She made many contributions to this field of study.

Awards / Honors

She was the first woman to serve as president of the American Physical Society.

She was awarded the National Medal of Science, the Comstock Prize, and the Wolf Prize.

She was the first woman to be awarded an honorary doctorate at Princeton University.

Her book Beta Decay, published in 1965, is still a standard reference for nuclear physicists.

Education

Chien-Shiung Wu attended a school started by her father, who believed in education for girls.

She was able to go on and study physics at a University in Shanghai.

In 1936, with some financial assistance from her uncle, she enrolled at the University of California, Berkley. She completed her Ph.D. in 1940.
